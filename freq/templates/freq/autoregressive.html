After subtracting trend and periodic components from the time series the residual should show stationarity. A stationary time series has the property that the mean, variance and autocorrelation structure do not change over time. Stationarity can be check by the autocorrelation analysis. If the values of the autocorrelation coefficient are placed within certain boundary (confidence limit -95%) the conclusion can be drawn that a seasonal component and trend have been successfully fitted and removed from a time series.
