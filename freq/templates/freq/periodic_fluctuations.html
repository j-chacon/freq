Groundwater levels often exhibit certain periodicity in fluctuations due to seasonally varying inputs (recharge/abstractions) into the groundwater system. A periodic component of groundwater time series can be estimated by harmonic analysis. The annual periodic fluctuation is often the most dominant one, but other fluctuation can also be present by varying abstraction rates or other seasonal fluctuations.

Number of significant periods of a time series can be estimated from the accumulated power spectrum. Once the number of periods is selected, the trend-free groundwater level time series will be fitted with a harmonic series.

The periodic fluctuations are subtracted from the time series to continue with the autoregressive model.
